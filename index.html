Butterfly Wing Aspect Ratio in Australia
Butterfly is one of the most common inserts. They help pollination and can be benefitial to agriculture making it valuable to study them. Wing aspect ratio is the metric I chose to study as it could be relatively easy to get from online butterfly images. The definition of wing aspect ratio in this study is the ratio of wing span and wing width. The bigger this value is, the longer and narrower the wing will be.

In this study, Heteronympha merope and Pieris Rapae, two special butterfly species, are chosen as they are one of the most common butterflies and provide more data.
Recorded Butterfly Locations
The above chart is a map showing both the location and wing aspect ratio of each recoding. Each circle represents a recording of the butterfly. The size of the circle represents its wing aspect ratio.

It is observed that they are near sea and concentrate on the south eastern part of Australia.
Wing Aspect Ratio Boxplot
From the boxplot, it is observed that the mean wing aspect ratio of Heteronympha merope is about 1.6 and the mean wing aspect ratio of Pieris Rapae is about 1.8. The wing aspect ratio range is wider for Heteronympha merope.
Number of Recorded Butterfly Bar Chart
From the bar chart, it is observed that the number of recordings increase significantly in the recent year. The reason for that might be with the help of peronal cellphones, it becomes easy and convenient for people to upload photos to the online database.
Wing Aspect Ratio & Weather
Intuitively, butterfly wing aspect ratio should relate to weather. As the wing gets bigger, it would become harder for them to fly in heavy wind area. The chart supports this expectation. It is observed from the wind speed heatmap, the number of recordings is much bigger when the wing aspect ratio is below 1.8.

Interestingly, it is oberved that wing aspect ratio below 1.8 seems more suitable for survival under different types of weather.
Wing Aspect Ratio & Geolocation
It is observed that the number of records concentrate when longtitude is around 145 and latitude is around -37 and decrease bilaterally.

Conclusion
The study shows that the nature does not favour excessively large or small butterfly wing aspect ratio. Under various conditions,butterflies of different wing aspect ratios can be observed. This reflects biodiversity.
